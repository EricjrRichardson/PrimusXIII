Jordan 1

The Jordan 1, introduced in 1985, marked the beginning of a basketball legacy. Designed by Peter Moore, its bold colors and high-top silhouette revolutionized the sneaker industry. Michael Jordan's inaugural signature shoe not only captured the essence of his athletic prowess but also became a cultural icon, transcending the court to become a symbol of self-expression and rebellion. Its notoriety skyrocketed when the NBA banned the original black and red colorway, known as the 'Bred' colorway, due to violating uniform regulations. This controversy only fueled the shoe's popularity, solidifying its place in sneaker history as an icon of defiance and style."
